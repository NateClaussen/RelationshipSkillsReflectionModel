Knapps Relationship Model Demo


Explanation

Using Knapps Relationship Model, we can analyze how relationships are started between two people and also how they can end. Using this website, you can use the "A" and "D" keys or the Left and right arrow keys to move the couple through each part of their relationship.

  1. Initiating Phase: This phase is where the relationship has the potential to be formed. It can happen in mere seconds on whether or not you engage with another person. If you choose to initiate with another person, then the relationship has officially begun!
  2. Experimenting Phase: This phase is where you explore the relationship further and see if there is a genuine connection. You can talk about common interests and start to see what works. A lot of times, this phase can be very surface level and does not get too deep.
  3. Intensifying Phase: This phase is where the relationship starts to become more serious. As the name suggests, this is very high enery and your relationship has started to kick off to a great start! You will start sharing personal stories and share experiences together.
  4. Integrating Phase: In this phase, you begin to start thinking of yourself as a couple and other people can see that you are together. You start considering yourself as "we" and "us" instead of just "I" and "Me".
  5. Bonding Phase: This phase is where you are connected with someone to the point where you are in a blissful happiness. This phase is usually denoted by a serious act, such as marriage, and at this point, you have a strong relationship!
  6. Differentiation Phase: After the honeymoon phase is over, you start to see things that you might not like. You realize that you don't like everything that the other person likes, and you start setting healthy boundaries and communicating openly with your parter.
  7. Circumscribing Phase: This phase is where you are still in a healthy relationship, but maybe there is a thing or two that is starting to build a wedge between the two of you. You have a choice to either address the concerns and improve the relationship back to Circumsribing, or you can let it deteriorate towards Stagnation.
  8. Stagnation Phase: This phase is where you start to get kind of numb to your dissagreements. You start avoiding conversations, especially ones about the conflicts that you are having. You start putting more and more distance between you and your partner.
  9. Avoiding Phase: In this phase, you do everything you can to just avoid the other person. You start making excuses to not be in the same place, and you really try to stop communicating altogether
  10. Terminating Phase: This phase is the end of the road for the relationship. This phase usually involves a final big action (depending on how serious the relationship ended up getting to), such as a breakup or divorce. You do not want to see the other person again, and the relationship is no more.
